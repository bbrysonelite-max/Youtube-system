# VOICE_FORMATTING_RULES.md
# Script Formatting Rules for ElevenLabs + HeyGen Rendering
# READ THIS BEFORE GENERATING ANY SCRIPT

---

## The Core Principle
The script is not written to be read. It is written to be heard.
Every formatting decision serves the voice clone, not the page.

---

## Punctuation as Performance Direction

| Mark | What it tells the voice | Example |
|---|---|---|
| ... (ellipsis) | Natural pause, let it breathe | "Here's what nobody tells you..." |
| — (em dash) | Emphasis, rhythm break | "This changes everything — and I mean everything." |
| ? mid-script | Rise in tone, engage listener | "You see what just happened there?" |
| . (period) | Full stop. Short sentence follows. | Always. |

---

## Sentence Structure Rules
1. Short sentences after long ones. Always.
2. Never more than 2 commas before a period.
3. Maximum 20 words per sentence. Flag and revise anything longer.
4. Rhetorical questions every 90 seconds minimum.
5. Flesch-Kincaid target: Grade 8 or below.

---

## What to Avoid
- Corporate language: leverage synergies, value proposition, utilize
- Filler phrases: "So basically...", "What I mean is...", "Kind of like..."
- Passive voice: "It was decided" → "I decided"
- Three consecutive sentences starting with "I"
- Walls of unbroken text — break every 2-3 sentences

## Script Structure Template

[HOOK — 0-15 sec]
Pattern interrupt. Bold claim, question, or shocking stat.
One or two sentences. Maximum impact.

[PROMISE — 15-30 sec]
What they will know or be able to do by the end.
Specific. Concrete. No vague promises.

[CREDIBILITY — 30-45 sec]
Why Brent is the one teaching this.
Story-based, not resume-based.

[CORE CONTENT — 3-8 min]
Three main points.
Each point: claim, then story or example, then takeaway.
Use [pause] between points.

[CTA — final 60 sec]
Based on video number in sequence (see CHANNEL_IDENTITY.md CTA Sequence).
Direct. Specific. One action only.
