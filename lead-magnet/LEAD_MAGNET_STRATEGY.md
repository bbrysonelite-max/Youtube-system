## NEWSLETTER NAME DROP — HOW TO SAY IT ON CAMERA

Avoid: "Check out my newsletter called Agentic Loadout."
(Sounds like you're reading a slide.)

Use this instead — say it casually, mid-breath:

> "I send out a free weekly thing called the Agentic Loadout.
> It's where I drop the tools, the setups, the actual agent builds —
> stuff I can't fit in a 10-minute video. Link's in the description."

Or the shorter version when time is tight:

> "It's in this week's Agentic Loadout — link below."

The word 'loadout' does the work. It implies gear, readiness, preparation.
Your audience understands that framing without needing it explained.

## STRATEGY NOTES

### Phase Alignment with CTA Tiers

Videos 1–10:
- No lead magnet mention in-video.
- Factory Blueprint linked silently in description as "free resource."
- This seeds early organic opt-ins without diluting the subscribe CTA.

Videos 11–20:
- Introduce Factory Blueprint verbally using CTA copy above.
- Tiger Claw waitlist appears alongside it.
- Goal: build a segmented list. Blueprint opt-ins = warm leads for Tiger Claw.

Videos 21+:
- Dual CTA: Factory Blueprint (free) + Tiger Claw (paid, $147/mo).
- Email sequence bridges the two automatically (see EMAIL_WELCOME_SEQUENCE.md).

### List Segmentation Recommendation

Tag all Factory Blueprint opt-ins in Beehiiv as:
  source=youtube | tier=blueprint | status=nurture

When Tiger Claw launches, send a single broadcast to this segment first.
They are your highest-intent buyers. They opted in from video content,
consumed a tool-focused asset, and received three emails about agent systems.

### One Rule

Never mention price in-video during videos 11–20.
The waitlist is curiosity-only. Price enters at video 21+.
